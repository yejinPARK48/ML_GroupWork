(a) [3 points] We collect a set of data on the top 500 firms in the United States. For each firm, we record the profit, the number of employees, the industry, and the CEO salary. We are interested in understanding which factors affect CEO salary.

  1. Regression problem. This is because the CEO salary is a continuous variable, so we are modeling a numeric outcome.
  2. Inference. This is because the goal is to understand which factors influence CEO salary, not to predict it.
  3. (n) = 500, (p) = 3(profit, the number of employees, and the industry)

(b) [3 points] We are considering releasing a new product and want to know whether it will be a success or a failure. We collect data on 20 similar products that were previously launched. For each product, we have recorded whether it was a success or failure, the price charged for the product, the marketing budget, the competition price, and ten other variables.

  1. Classification problem. This is because the target variable is whether a product is a success or failure, which is categorical.
  2. Prediction. This is because the goal is to predict whether a new product will be a success or a failure.
  3. (n) = 20, (p) = 13(the price charged for the product, the marketing budget, the competition price, and 10 other variables)

(c) [3 points] We are interested in predicting the percentage change in the USD/Euro exchange rate in relation to the weekly changes in the world stock markets. Hence, we collect weekly data for all of 2012. For each week, we record the percent change in the USD/Euro, the precent change in the US market, the percent change in the British market, and the percent change in the German market.

  1. Regression problem. This is because the target variable is the percentage change in the USD/Euro exchange rate, which is a continuous variable.
  2. Prediction. This is becuase the goal is to predict the exchange rate change.
  3. (n) = 52(weeks in 2012), (p) = 3(the percent change in the US market, the percent change in the British market, and the percent change in the Germen market.
